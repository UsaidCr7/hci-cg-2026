Norman vs. GOMS Comparison Table – Go Dutch Payment/Split-wise

Comparison of Norman’s stages and GOMS concepts for splitting a meal payment
Norman’s Stage Go Dutch Payment/Split-wise GOMS Interpretation & Rationale Alignment Analysis
Goal ↔ GOAL User wants to pay only for what they ate, or ensure the total is split fairly among all attendees. Goal: Fair-Payment.
Top-level objective in GOMS.		 ✅ Direct correspondence – Norman’s goal aligns with GOMS goal.
Plan ↔ METHOD & Selection Rules User decides method: pay individually, split equally, or use an app to calculate shares. Considers fairness, convenience, and speed. Method: Identify each person’s order → calculate share → pay appropriate amount.
Selection Rules: Use “split by item” if detailed bill is available; else “equal split.” 		✅ Conceptual match – Norman’s planning stage corresponds to GOMS method selection.
Specify Determine the exact cost per person; verify items ordered; check rounding or discounts. Not explicitly modeled in basic GOMS.
Operators ([K] typing, [P] pointing) represent low-level input for calculations or app use. 		⚠️ Divergence – Norman emphasizes specification and verification; GOMS abstracts into operators.
Execute ↔ OPERATOR Enter amounts in payment app or terminal; confirm payment; distribute receipts if necessary. Operators: Mouse clicks, keystrokes; KLM can estimate timing of each primitive action. ✅ Direct match – execution stage aligns with GOMS operators.
Perceive / Interpret / Evaluate Observe confirmation; check that each person paid correctly; verify no one overpaid or underpaid. GOMS assumes perfect execution and does not allocate cognitive evaluation time. ⚠️ Divergence – Norman emphasizes feedback and evaluation; GOMS abstracts perception and evaluation.
